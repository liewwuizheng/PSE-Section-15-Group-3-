# Sprint 1: System Foundation
* **Duration:** Week 2
* **Goal:** Establish core user infrastructure and appointment management system.

---

## Scope of Work
To implement the system's basic functions, the following high-priority basic functions have been selected:

* **Account Security:** Patient registration, login/logout, and input verification.

* **Patient Appointment Flow:** Appointment, viewing, and cancellation/reschedulling interfaces.

* **Doctor's Daily View:** Display of doctors' daily schedules.

* **Admin Management:** A control panel was created for administrators to monitor, modify, and approve appointment records for hospital-wide coordination.

---

## Challenges and Solutions
| Challenges | Impact | Solutions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Role Integration** | Manage conflicting data needs among patients, physicians, and administrators. | Conduct collaborative process reviews to ensure data consistency across all three independent user interfaces.
| **User Experience Architecture** | Choose between complex single-page dashboards and modular navigation. | Simplify the structure by grouping related actions into logical modules, prioritizing usability over complexity.
| **Time Constraints** | Tight deadlines for high-fidelity user interface design and documentation. | Streamline coordination processes using **Trello** and assign responsibilities based on individual domain expertise.

---

## Retrospective
## Sprint Retrospective

### What Went Well
*   **Rapid Prototyping:** UI sketches were completed ahead of schedule, providing a clear visual roadmap for development.
*   **Structural Integrity:** Class and Use Case diagrams accurately mapped out system relationships, preventing logic errors early on.
*   **Team Communication:** Roles were well-defined, and the team maintained a high level of transparency through daily syncs.

### Areas for Improvement
*   **Testing Bottleneck:** The test plan was finalized later than intended, causing a slight delay in validation.
*   **Task Synchronization:** Some development tasks moved faster than the testing phase, leading to minor out-of-order execution.

---

## Continuous Process Improvement (CPI)
For **Sprint 2**, the team will implement the following:
*   **Shift-Left Testing:** Prepare test cases *during* the prototype phase to allow for immediate validation.
*   **Reusable Components:** Develop UI templates to accelerate the design of future clinical features.
*   **Active Trello Management:** Shift to real-time progress updates to reduce reliance on PO reminders.
